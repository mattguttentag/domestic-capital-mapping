# Multi-Vehicle Research Audit - 2026-06-25

This memo reviews whether two aggregate vehicle rows should be split into separate fund-specific rows:

- `Helios Investors II/III/IV (historical)`
- `Capital Alliance Private Equity I-IV (CAPE)`

The inclusion standard used here is stricter than a manager/fund-family relationship: a separate vehicle should be included only where public evidence ties African PF/SWF capital to that specific fund, or where the evidence is a clearly identified pension/SWF aggregate for that named fund.

## Summary Recommendation

| Fund family | Split into separate vehicles? | Recommendation |
|---|---:|---|
| Helios Investors II/III/IV | No, not on current public evidence | Remove or move the aggregate Helios II/III/IV row to a reviewed/reference category unless fund-specific PIC/GEPF LP evidence is found. Do not count Helios II, III, or IV as confirmed African PF/SWF commitments. |
| CAPE I-IV | Partially | Split CAPE III and CAPE IV if aggregate Nigerian pension-fund participation is acceptable. Do not keep `CAPE I-IV` as one vehicle. CAPE I and CAPE II should not be included as African PF/SWF commitments on current evidence. |

## Helios Investors II / III / IV

### Findings

Public sources confirm that Helios II, III, and IV were real Africa-focused private equity funds and had DFI participation, especially IFC and BII/CDC. However, I did not find public fund-level evidence that PIC/GEPF or another African PF/SWF was an LP in Helios II, III, or IV.

The evidence currently in the workbook says PIC has been a recurring LP since Helios II, but the linked source is Helios' investments page and does not substantiate that claim at the fund-LP level. Search results did surface a clear 2015 GEPF/PIC investment in Bayport, but that is a direct portfolio-company investment alongside/into a Helios portfolio context, not proof that GEPF was an LP in Helios II, III, or IV.

## Capital Alliance Private Equity I-IV (CAPE)

### Findings

The CAPE family should not remain as a single `CAPE I-IV` vehicle row. Public evidence is strongest for CAPE III and CAPE IV:

- CAPE III: An EMPEA interview with ACA's Okechukwu Enelamah states ACA received about US$45m from local pension funds into its third fund.
- CAPE IV: Multiple sources support pension-fund involvement. GPCA/ACA says CAPE IV investors included public and private pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, funds of funds, and DFIs. BII's mobilisation report says BII's CAPE IV investment helped create a local-currency-enabled vehicle that attracted local pension funds. PenCom's 2020 annual report says CPFA private-equity investments increased due to capital calls on Capital Alliance Private Equity Fund IV.

Evidence for CAPE I and CAPE II shows real funds and DFI participation, but I did not find African pension/SWF LP evidence for those specific funds.

| CAPE III | ACA/EMPEA interview says about US$45m came from local pension funds into the third fund. | Yes, aggregate local pension funds; individual PFAs not named. | Yes, if aggregate Nigerian pension-fund participation is acceptable. Confidence: Medium. |

### Recommended Dataset Treatment

Replace the aggregate CAPE rows with fund-specific treatment:

1. Remove `Capital Alliance Private Equity I-IV (CAPE)` from Vehicles & Instruments as a single row.
2. Replace the aggregate `CAPE I-IV` commitment row with:
   - `Capital Alliance Private Equity III (CAPE III)`
   - `Capital Alliance Private Equity IV (CAPE IV)`
3. Treat LP names as aggregate where public sources do not name individual PFAs:
   - `Nigerian local pension funds (aggregate; individual PFAs not publicly named)` for CAPE III.
   - `Nigerian PFAs / CPFAs (aggregate; individual PFAs not publicly named)` for CAPE IV.
4. Use `Confidence = M` for CAPE III and `Confidence = M/H` for CAPE IV depending on whether the database accepts aggregate PenCom category disclosures as high-confidence.
5. Do not add CAPE I or CAPE II as African PF/SWF-backed vehicles unless new evidence names African pension/SWF LPs.

## Immediate Data-Cleanup Implications

- `Helios Investors II/III/IV (historical)` should be removed from confirmed commitment and vehicle counts.
- `Capital Alliance Private Equity I-IV (CAPE)` should be split; the current aggregate row should not remain as a vehicle.
- The NSIA row `Capital Alliance Private Equity (CAPE) funds` remains weak unless a source is found tying NSIA to a specific ACA/CAPE fund. It should be reviewed separately from the Nigerian PFA/CPFA aggregate evidence.
